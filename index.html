Creative Statement

My work is all about interpretation. The idea that there is more than what meets the eye. I gain inspiration from the genre of impressionism art, adopting the styles of artists such as Edgar Degas. I portray this concept by capturing fleeting, real-life moments and sometimes blurry and 'unblended' photographs. I admire still settings, where seemingly nothing is happening, moments that may look like they may be better as the background of a portrait. I find meaning behind the simple, knowing that there is always more to see. I aim for both vibrant and apathetic moods when working with any medium, emphasizing light, or the absence of it, displaying the simplicity yet elegance of everyday life.
